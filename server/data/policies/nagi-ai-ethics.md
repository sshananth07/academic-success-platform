# National AI Governance & Innovation (NAGI) Framework
## Education Sector Guidelines: Responsible AI Use in Malaysian Higher Education
**Ministry of Digital, Malaysia | 2024 Edition**

---

## 1. Overview

The National AI Governance & Innovation (NAGI) Framework is Malaysia's primary governance instrument for the responsible deployment and use of artificial intelligence across all sectors. This Education Sector Supplement provides specific guidance for higher education institutions (HEIs), academic staff, and students on the ethical use of AI in educational contexts.

These guidelines align with Malaysia's National AI Roadmap 2021–2025 and the ASEAN Guide on AI Governance and Ethics. Malaysian public universities are required to demonstrate alignment with NAGI principles as part of their institutional governance reporting to the Ministry of Higher Education (MOHE).

---

## 2. The Six Principles of Responsible AI in Education

### Principle 1: Transparency (Ketelusan)

AI tools used in educational contexts must be transparent in their nature, capabilities, and limitations.

**For students:** When AI tools are used in preparing academic work, students must disclose this use clearly. Transparency means not concealing the role of AI in producing submitted work. Using AI tools and presenting the output as purely one's own is a violation of the transparency principle.

**For institutions:** HEIs must be transparent about which AI tools they deploy (e.g., plagiarism detection AI, grading assistance tools), how these tools work, what data they collect, and how results are used in academic decisions.

**For AI tools themselves:** Institutions should prefer AI tools that provide explanations for their outputs (explainability), particularly when AI is used in assessment contexts. Black-box AI tools with no explainability should not be used for high-stakes academic decisions.

### Principle 2: Fairness (Keadilan)

AI tools in education must not perpetuate or amplify discrimination, bias, or inequality.

**Bias assessment:** Institutions deploying AI in assessment must assess these tools for potential bias against students of particular demographic groups, language backgrounds, or learning styles. Tools found to produce systematically biased outcomes must not be used without bias mitigation measures.

**Access equity:** AI governance must account for differential access to AI tools. Students from B40 (bawah 40 peratus) households or rural communities may have limited access to advanced AI tools. Policies must not disadvantage students who lack access to AI tools that are beneficial and permitted in coursework.

**Language fairness:** AI tools used in Malaysian HEIs must demonstrate adequate capability in Bahasa Malaysia and should not disadvantage students who submit work in Bahasa Malaysia compared to those submitting in English.

### Principle 3: Accountability (Akauntabiliti)

Clear accountability frameworks must exist for AI use in educational contexts.

**Institutional accountability:** HEIs are accountable for AI tools they deploy or recommend. If an institution-approved AI tool causes harm (e.g., incorrect plagiarism detection leading to wrongful misconduct findings), the institution bears responsibility for remediation.

**Student accountability:** Students are accountable for the content they submit, regardless of whether AI tools were used in its production. The use of AI does not transfer authorship responsibility or academic accountability to the AI system.

**Developer accountability:** Institutions should prefer AI tools from developers who maintain transparency reports, publish bias assessments, and operate under identifiable legal entities subject to Malaysian or internationally recognised jurisdiction.

### Principle 4: Privacy (Privasi)

Student data privacy must be protected in all AI-related educational activities.

**Data minimisation:** AI tools used in education should collect only the minimum student data necessary for their function. Tools that harvest student writing samples for model training without explicit consent are not permitted under NAGI guidelines.

**PDPA compliance:** All AI tools processing student personal data must comply with Malaysia's Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA). Student assignment content, performance data, and demographic information are classified as personal data under PDPA.

**Storage and retention:** Student data processed by AI tools must not be retained by third-party AI providers beyond the period necessary for the tool's function, unless explicit, informed consent has been obtained.

**Cross-border transfer:** Transfer of Malaysian student data to AI servers located outside Malaysia must comply with PDPA cross-border transfer requirements. Institutions must maintain records of international data transfer arrangements.

### Principle 5: Human Oversight (Pengawasan Manusia)

AI tools in education must remain under meaningful human oversight. AI must support — not replace — human judgment in academic decisions.

**Assessment decisions:** Final academic assessment decisions (grading, progression, graduation clearance, academic misconduct findings) must be made by qualified human academic staff. AI may assist in these processes but may not be the sole or decisive factor.

**Misconduct investigations:** AI-generated plagiarism or misconduct flags must be reviewed by human investigators before any formal action is taken. A student may not be penalised solely on the basis of an AI detection system's output.

**Grievance access:** Students affected by AI-assisted academic decisions must have access to a human reviewer who can explain the basis for the decision and receive and consider the student's response.

**Override capability:** Institutions must maintain the technical and procedural capability to override, correct, or reverse AI-generated decisions or recommendations.

### Principle 6: Inclusivity (Inklusiviti)

AI use in education must support inclusive learning and not create or deepen educational inequity.

**Accessibility:** AI tools used in instructional contexts must be accessible to students with disabilities. Where AI tools have accessibility limitations, equivalent non-AI alternatives must be available.

**Digital literacy support:** HEIs have an obligation to provide AI literacy training to students, particularly those from underrepresented or disadvantaged backgrounds, so that all students can navigate AI-assisted academic environments competently and ethically.

**Rural and low-bandwidth contexts:** AI tools recommended for student use must function adequately on low-bandwidth internet connections common in rural Malaysian communities. Institutions should maintain offline or low-data alternatives for critical academic support functions.

## 3. Student Data Privacy Requirements

The following minimum standards apply to all AI tools processing student data in HEIs:

3.1 Institutions must maintain a data processing register for all AI tools that process student data.
3.2 Data processing agreements must be in place with all third-party AI tool providers.
3.3 Students must be informed which AI tools process their data, for what purpose, and for how long.
3.4 Students must have the right to request deletion of their data from AI systems, subject to legitimate retention requirements.
3.5 AI tools must not use student academic work to train commercial AI models without explicit, freely given, informed consent from the student.

---

## 4. AI Tool Vetting Process for Institutions

Before recommending or mandating AI tool use by students, HEIs must complete a vetting process that includes:

4.1 **Privacy impact assessment (PIA)** — evaluating data collection, processing, storage, and retention practices.
4.2 **Bias and fairness review** — testing tool outputs across diverse student demographics and language backgrounds.
4.3 **Terms of service review** — ensuring terms do not conflict with student rights or institutional obligations.
4.4 **Security assessment** — confirming appropriate cybersecurity controls.
4.5 **Accessibility review** — confirming WCAG 2.1 AA compliance or equivalent.

Tools that fail any vetting criterion may not be added to the institution's approved AI tools list.

---

## 5. Prohibited AI Applications in Assessment

The following AI applications are prohibited in academic assessment contexts under NAGI guidelines:

5.1 **Automated final grading** — using AI to assign final grades without human review and confirmation.
5.2 **Biometric proctoring without consent** — using AI facial recognition, eye-tracking, or keystroke dynamics analysis without explicit informed student consent.
5.3 **Predictive academic misconduct profiling** — using AI to flag students as "high misconduct risk" based on demographic or behavioural patterns rather than specific evidence of misconduct.
5.4 **AI-generated misconduct findings** — treating AI plagiarism detection output as conclusive evidence of misconduct without human review of the specific flagged content.

---

## 6. Academic Staff AI Literacy Requirements

NAGI guidelines recommend that HEIs require all academic staff to complete a minimum of 8 hours of AI literacy training annually, covering:

- Fundamentals of how generative AI tools work
- Identification of AI-generated content in student submissions
- Ethical use of AI in teaching preparation and research
- Data privacy obligations when using AI tools
- Assessment design strategies that promote academic integrity in an AI era

---

## 7. Reporting Obligations

HEIs must report annually to MOHE on:

7.1 AI tools deployed institutionally, with justification and vetting status
7.2 Incidents of AI-related data privacy breaches
7.3 Academic misconduct cases involving AI misuse
7.4 Student AI literacy training completion rates
7.5 Bias or fairness concerns identified with deployed AI tools

---

## 8. Alignment with Malaysia's National AI Roadmap

These guidelines implement the Education pillar of Malaysia's National AI Roadmap 2021–2025, which identifies the following priorities:

- Building AI-ready human capital through ethical AI education
- Ensuring AI development and deployment respects Malaysian values and cultural context
- Promoting inclusive AI access across income and geographic divides
- Establishing governance frameworks that enable innovation while protecting individual rights

HEIs that demonstrate leadership in responsible AI governance in education may apply for NAGI recognition as an AI-Ready Institution (Institusi Sedia AI), which carries reputational benefits and eligibility for certain MOHE innovation grants.
