# Universiti Awam Malaysia — Artificial Intelligence Use Policy for Academic Work
**Version 3.1 | Effective: 1 January 2025 | Office of Academic Affairs**

---

## 1. Introduction and Purpose

Universiti Awam Malaysia (UAM) recognises that artificial intelligence (AI) tools are increasingly embedded in everyday academic life. This policy establishes clear guidelines for the responsible, transparent, and ethical use of AI tools in all forms of academic work submitted at UAM. The policy applies to all students (undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral), academic staff, and research personnel affiliated with UAM.

The goal of this policy is not to prohibit AI use outright, but to ensure that AI tools serve as supplements to — not substitutes for — genuine intellectual effort, critical thinking, and original contribution. Academic integrity (integriti akademik) remains the foundational principle of all scholarly work at UAM.

---

## 2. Definitions

**AI Tool:** Any software, application, or system that uses machine learning, natural language processing, or generative models to produce text, images, code, or other outputs. This includes but is not limited to ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Microsoft Copilot, Claude, Grammarly Premium, and similar tools.

**AI-Generated Content:** Any text, data, image, code, or other material primarily produced by an AI tool, regardless of subsequent editing by a human.

**AI-Assisted Work:** Work where a human author uses AI tools for specific sub-tasks (e.g., grammar checking, brainstorming, literature searching) while retaining primary authorship of the intellectual content.

**Substantive AI Use:** Use of AI tools to generate significant portions of academic content that is then submitted as the student's own work.

---

## 3. Permitted Uses of AI Tools

The following uses of AI tools are explicitly permitted, subject to proper disclosure:

3.1 **Grammar and language checking** — using tools like Grammarly to correct spelling, punctuation, and grammatical errors in work that the student has authored.

3.2 **Brainstorming and idea generation** — using AI to generate a list of topic ideas or research angles that the student then independently evaluates and develops.

3.3 **Literature search assistance** — using AI to suggest search terms, identify research databases, or locate sources, provided the student independently reads and critically evaluates each source.

3.4 **Paraphrasing support** — using AI to suggest alternative phrasings of passages the student has already written, when the student retains the original meaning and intellectual contribution.

3.5 **Translation assistance** — for students whose primary language is not Bahasa Malaysia or English, AI translation tools may be used to assist comprehension, provided the submitted work is the student's own intellectual product.

3.6 **Code debugging** — in computer science and engineering assignments where code debugging assistance is permitted by the course instructor.

3.7 **Data analysis** — using AI-assisted statistical tools for analysis, where the research design, interpretation, and conclusions are the student's own.

---

## 4. Prohibited Uses of AI Tools

The following uses of AI tools are strictly prohibited and constitute academic misconduct (salah laku akademik):

4.1 **Submitting AI-generated text as original work** — generating an essay, report, assignment, or any substantial portion thereof using AI and submitting it as the student's own intellectual product without disclosure.

4.2 **AI-assisted contract cheating** — using AI tools to complete work on behalf of another student, or having another person use AI tools to complete work submitted in the student's name.

4.3 **AI-generated data fabrication** — using AI tools to generate fictitious research data, experimental results, or survey responses.

4.4 **Undisclosed AI use in assessments** — using AI tools during examinations, tests, quizzes, or any timed assessment unless explicitly permitted by the course instructor.

4.5 **AI paraphrasing to circumvent plagiarism detection** — using AI tools specifically to rephrase plagiarised material so that it evades plagiarism detection software.

4.6 **AI-generated citations** — including fabricated references, DOIs, or sources generated by AI that do not correspond to real published works.

---

## 5. Mandatory Disclosure Requirements

Students must declare AI tool usage in all assessments and submissions where AI was used in any substantive way. The disclosure statement must:

5.1 **Specify which AI tool(s) were used** (e.g., "This assignment used Google Gemini for grammar correction").

5.2 **Describe the nature of AI use** (e.g., brainstorming, language polishing, literature search).

5.3 **Be included in the submission** — typically as an appendix, footnote, or dedicated section labelled "AI Use Declaration."

The UAM standard disclosure format is:

> "In preparing this work, I used [AI Tool Name] for [specific purpose]. All substantive analysis, argumentation, interpretation, and conclusions are my own."

Failure to disclose known AI use is treated as an act of deception and may result in elevated penalties under Section 7.

---

## 6. How to Cite AI-Generated Content

When AI-generated content is legitimately incorporated into academic work (with instructor permission and proper disclosure), it must be cited in APA 7th Edition format. Refer to the APA Citation Guide for AI citation examples.

General format:
> OpenAI. (2024). *ChatGPT* (GPT-4 version) [Large language model]. https://chat.openai.com

---

## 7. Consequences of Violations — Four-Tier Penalty Framework

UAM applies a graduated penalty framework for AI-related academic misconduct, based on severity and repetition:

**Tier 1 — Verbal Warning and Remedial Action:**
First-time minor violations (e.g., undisclosed AI grammar use in a low-stakes assignment). The student meets with the course instructor, completes an academic integrity module, and resubmits the work.

**Tier 2 — Written Warning and Grade Penalty:**
Moderate violations (e.g., substantive AI-generated content in a coursework assignment without disclosure, first offence). A formal written warning is issued, the assignment receives a failing grade (0%), and the incident is recorded in the student's academic file.

**Tier 3 — Course Failure and Mandatory Counselling:**
Serious violations (e.g., AI-generated final report, repeated Tier 2 offences). The student fails the entire course, must attend an academic integrity counselling programme, and the Faculty Academic Integrity Committee (FAIC) conducts a formal review.

**Tier 4 — Suspension or Expulsion:**
Severe or repeated violations (e.g., AI-assisted contract cheating, data fabrication, Tier 3 repeat offences). The Senate Academic Integrity Board (SAIB) convenes a formal hearing. Outcomes may include suspension for one to two semesters, permanent transcript notation of academic misconduct, or expulsion from UAM. Expelled students may not re-enrol at any public university in Malaysia for five years.

---

## 8. Faculty-Specific Variations

Individual faculties may establish additional restrictions on AI tool use within their programmes, provided these restrictions are not less stringent than this policy. Specific variations must be:

- Published in the faculty student handbook
- Communicated to students in writing at the start of each semester
- Consistent with UAM's overarching academic integrity framework

---

## 9. Approved AI Tools List

UAM maintains a list of AI tools that have been vetted for data privacy compliance. Students should consult the UAM Academic Affairs Portal for the current approved list. Using non-approved tools that process and store student work on unvetted external servers may constitute a data privacy violation in addition to any academic misconduct.

---

## 10. Examination and Timed Assessment Rules

AI tools are prohibited in all examinations and timed assessments unless the course instructor explicitly specifies otherwise in writing. "Open AI" examinations must clearly state which tools are permitted and under what conditions. Students found using unpermitted AI tools during examinations are subject to immediate Tier 3 or Tier 4 penalties depending on circumstances.

---

## 11. Appeals Process

Students who receive academic misconduct findings under this policy have the right to appeal within 14 calendar days of receiving the written decision. Appeals must be submitted to the Faculty Academic Affairs Office in writing, stating the grounds for appeal. A separate Appeals Panel, not including the original decision-makers, will review the appeal within 21 calendar days.
